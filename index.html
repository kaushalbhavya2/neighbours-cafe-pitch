Brand atmosphere

The courtyard, mural, and greenery are part of the product. They make the cafe feel distinct before the food even hits the table.

Client story

Neighbours already has the product. The next 90 days are about making it visible, memorable, and easy to choose.

The Opportunity

Good cafe. Under-signalled advantage.

The markdown strategy is clear: Neighbours does not have a food problem. It has a visibility problem, plus a few first-visit barriers that are costing weekday traffic every week.

01

The hidden asset

A sunny courtyard, 80-seat capacity, dog-friendly setup, and a menu with memorable dishes already give Neighbours a strong local story.

02

The actual problem

Google Business Profile is under-optimised, Instagram is inconsistent, and there is no proper branded website to help first-time visitors decide.

03

The commercial unlock

Dog owners, remote workers, and local brunch customers are high-frequency segments. Once they visit and like the experience, retention becomes much easier.

Core Pitch

Position Neighbours as the neighbourhood’s outdoor living room.

A relaxed, dog-welcome cafe where locals settle in, feel known, and come back because the place already feels like theirs.

That idea should shape every touchpoint over the next three months: Google copy, signage, website hierarchy, campaign launches, staff scripts, and the content mix.

This is the kind of real, generous, colourful food story we want the brand to lead with: memorable plates, sunshine, and an everyday brunch experience worth coming back for.
The Rules

The 3 non-negotiables for the first 90 days.

These are the actions that need to happen first. If they are not done consistently, the rest of the plan should not scale.

Discovery

Google Business Profile overhaul

Update the description, upload fresh courtyard and dog images, confirm key attributes, and add real menu items with prices.

  • Do this in Week 1
  • Target searches: dog-friendly cafe, courtyard cafe, brunch in St Kilda
  • Track direction requests from the Day 1 baseline
Street Signal

Dog welcome visible from the footpath

Move the water bowl to the gate and add simple handwritten signage so passing dog owners instantly understand they are invited in.

  • Install on Day 1
  • No discount required
  • Turns the thesis into something physical
Content Habit

Instagram at 3 posts per week

Delegate capture to staff, keep the owner on light approvals, and build the content rhythm before asking the channel to perform.

  • Courtyard + dogs
  • Signature dishes with price cues
  • Warm, familiar, unforced tone
90-Day Plan

Three months. One clear growth story.

The first quarter is not about trying everything. It is about sequencing the right moves so the venue becomes easier to find, easier to choose, and easier to return to.

    Channel Stack

    What this plan actually uses.

    Everything here is realistic for a single-location cafe. Each channel has one job, and each one supports weekday foot traffic rather than vanity metrics.

    Google + Local SEO

    Capture intent

    Show up for local searches when customers are already deciding where to go.

    • Rewrite the listing around dogs, courtyard, and Chapel Street location
    • Upload new photos early
    • Push reviews mentioning dogs and atmosphere
    Instagram

    Make the place feel alive

    Use saves and profile visits as the real metric, not likes.

    • 3 posts per week
    • Lead with courtyard, dogs, Chilli Hash, Shakshuka
    • Show prices and real tables, not over-styled content
    Website

    Convert interest into first visits

    Give people a mobile-friendly menu, clear dog-friendly proof, and directions fast.

    • Six simple pages
    • HTML menu for SEO, not a PDF
    • Dog-friendly page as a conversion page
    CRM + In-store

    Turn first-timers into regulars

    The Regulars loyalty card creates the first retention loop immediately.

    • QR capture on cards, tables, and receipts
    • Track dog-owner status for future relevance
    • Use ritual-based messaging, not discount spam

    Month 3 success gate

    The first 90 days need three clear pass/fail checks before heavier scaling happens.

    • Weekday covers: up 15% from the Week 1 baseline
    • GBP direction requests: up 20% from the Day 1 screenshot
    • Instagram cadence: 3 posts every week, not averaged later

    Secondary KPI signals

    • Loyalty sign-ups: 10 to 15 new sign-ups per week by the end of the quarter
    • 30-day return rate: at least 40% of new card holders come back
    • Instagram saves: average 10+ saves per post, with at least 2 posts per month above 20 saves
    Why Data Matters

    The data is what turns this from a nice plan into a revenue plan.

    To grow weekday traffic properly, we need access to the numbers that show what is actually happening now. Sales data, POS reporting, covers, average spend, and item-level sales let us make decisions based on evidence rather than guesswork.

    Why We Need It

    We need a real baseline before we can claim growth.

    Without sales and POS data, there is no reliable starting point. That makes it impossible to prove whether the plan is lifting weekday traffic or just creating activity.

    • Weekday covers show whether traffic is actually increasing
    • Average spend shows whether customers are buying enough once they arrive
    • Day-by-day sales reveal which mornings need the most help
    What It Unlocks

    We can identify exactly where the revenue opportunity sits.

    The data tells us which products, times, and customer habits are worth pushing hardest in marketing and in-store messaging.

    • Item sales show which high-margin dishes are underperforming and need visibility
    • Time-of-day trends show where quiet periods can be turned into rituals
    • Channel attribution helps us see whether Google, Instagram, loyalty, or local drops are driving visits
    Commercial Value

    It helps us spend smarter, not just market harder.

    Better data means the budget can be concentrated on what is actually moving covers and repeat visits instead of being spread thinly across ideas.

    • Back winning dishes with more content and better placement
    • Double down on the weekday windows that respond best
    • Stop activities that look busy but do not change revenue
    What We Need From The Client

    Simple access is enough to make the strategy much sharper.

    We do not need a complicated data project. A clean weekly export or basic reporting access is enough to make this plan measurable and commercially accountable.

    • Weekly sales by day
    • POS covers and average order value
    • Top and bottom selling menu items
    • Any existing loyalty, email, or customer history available
    Client Decision

    What needs approval today.

    This is intentionally simple. The client only needs to commit to a few operational decisions for the plan to become real.

    Operational

    Nominate one owner

    One person needs to approve content weekly, own the calendar, and capture the baseline numbers from day one.

    Data Access

    Share POS and sales reporting

    Weekday sales, covers, average spend, and item-level reporting are what let us measure impact properly and optimise the plan as we go.

    Production

    Book the first 2-hour shoot

    Measurement

    Review the scoreboard weekly

    Weekday covers, direction requests, sign-ups, review count, and Instagram saves are enough to show what is working and what needs adjusting.
